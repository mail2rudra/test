Getting Physically active helps you in:
  • Controlling your weight
  • Reduce your risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes and some cancers
  • Strengthen your bones and muscles
  • Improve your mental health and mood

What you need to do:
  • If you are between Age 5-17, exercise in a moderate to rigorous level once daily
  • If you are of age 18-64, exercise moderately for 30 minutes and add 15 minutes of rigorous activity once daily
  • If you are age 65 and above you should continue doing the same form of exercise but adjust to your physcal or medical conditions as recommended by doctors if you cannot do the recommeded amouts of physical activity due to your health conditions, you should be as physically active as our conditions allow.
  • If you are above the age of 17, you should also do muscle-strengthening activities 2 or more days a week.
  • For additional health and well being benefits practise Yoga along with the recommended physical activites.

Please Remember:
  • There is no golden rule. Moderate exercise is the activity that that elicits a slight increase in breathing, while still allowing you to talk comfortably. Common moderate exercise could be brisk walking, doing household chores, gardening etc
  • Rigorous exercise are those activities that elicits higher increase in the breathing, and while doing so, You may not be able to converse more than a few words without passing a breath – (running, hopping, skipping, jumping rope, swimming, playing sports, dancing, bicycling etc.
  • You can include Muscle strengthening activities by using (I AM LOST AT THIS, WILL WORK ON IT LATER)
  • IF YOU HAVE UNDERLYING HEALTH CONDITION AND ARE ABOVE THE AGE OF 65 ALWAYS CONSULT YOUR DOCTOR BEFORE DOING RIGOROUS EXERCISE
