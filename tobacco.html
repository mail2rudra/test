When you smoke cigarettes, it

  • Causes lung cancer and many other types of cancer, Heart disease, Stroke, Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and other lung diseases
  • It also causes Pregnancy problems, Gum disease, Vision loss, diabetes, Rheumatoid arthritis
  • It also causes bad breath, bad taste in mouth, yellow and brown stains on teeth, lost athletic ability, elevated heart rate and Chronic cough.

When you smoke, you are alos causing harm to your children, family and people around you
In babies and children, breating in secondhand smoke can cause:

  • Sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS)
  • Pneumonia, Bronchitis, Servere asthama attacks and Ear infections

Quitting all forms of tobacco use is by far the best health decsion that you can ever make. Smoking hurts almost every part of your body.
If you use other form of tobacco (using Khaini, Gutkha and Paan etc):

  • YOu have significant risk of developing Mouth, tounge, cheek, and gum cancer, Cancer in the esophagus (the swalowing tube that goes form your mouth to your stomach) and Pancreatic cancer.
  • It also cause ulcers in mouth, permanent gum loss and damage to your teeth.

What you need to do:

Quitting is hard, but millions of people have done it successfully. Nicotine - the drug found in tobacco - is just as addictive as heroin or cocaine. It's the nicotine in cigarettes that causes the strong feeling that you want to smoke (craving). Remember - quitting isn't easy, but it is possible.
Quitting smoking can help you:

  • Your body begins to heal as soon as you quit smoking. You will breathe more easily; your senses of taste and smell will improve.
  • You will have more energy, your lungs will beome stronger, making it easier for you to be active and you will cough less.
  • Lower your risk of developing chronic desease (heart attack, cancer or stroke) and you will live a longer, healthier life.

Alcohol Use:

Contrary to what was widely believed in the past, newer evidence suggests that there is no health benefit of drinking any amount of alcohol. Even though drining alcohol in moderation does not poses serious health risks among healthy adults, even small amount of alcohol consumption carres some risks.
Quitting drinking alcohol can help you:

  • Lower your blood pressure and Lower your risk of injury
  • Lower your risk of developing habit of alcohol abuse
  • Avoid health problems like heart disease, stroke, some types of cancer, and liver problems.
  • Helps you Lose weight and also SAVES YOU MONEY

Take these steps to help reduce your alcohol consumption:
  • Limit use of alcohol to 2 drinks or less
  • Make timetable to quit drinking for good of you have underlying health condition.
  • Understand if you already have a drinking problem by thinking if it causes trouble with your relationshiops, family and work.
  • If you think you have a drinking problem, don't hide it, share it with family and friends and seed help from doctor, and or Rehabilitation centers.
  • REMEMBER if you have a drinking problem, it may not always be in your control to quit, SEEK HELP.

Please Remember:


You risk serious harm if you drink alcohol while
  • You are pregnant or trying to get pregnant
  • You have a health condition that can be made worse by drinking (like liver disease) and
  • You take certain over-the-counter or prescription medicines that cross reacts with alcohol (check the medicine label)
  • You are recovering from alcoholism
  • You are going to drive a car or use a machine, hike, swim etc.
