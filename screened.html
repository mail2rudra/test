  • Unlike diagnostic tests which are usually done to find out what is causing certain symptoms (mostly referred by a doctor); Screening tests are done in people who do not necessarily feel ill.
  • Screening aims to detect diseases at an early stage, before any symptoms become noticeable.
  • This has the advantage of being able to treat the disease much earlier, improving chance of cure, and or prevent progression of diseases, and thus prolonging life and minimizing cost of treatment.
  • Getting recommended screening tests is an important thing you can do for your health. You’ll need certain screening tests depending on your age, sex and health conditions. At Minimum, the Ministry of Health Nepal recommends screening for ( please refer to the table for age specific recommendation)
  1. Blood Pressure
  2. Blood glucose level
  3. Lipid profile
  4. Monitor you Body Mass Index (BMI)
  5. And physical examination

Please Consult your health service provider for other screening and routine tests.

  • Blood Pressure

    Why or What is the risk?

    Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) is the important risk factor for Cardiovascular Disease(Heart Attack), Cerebrovascular Disease (Stroke), Renal Failure, Peripheral Vascular Disease,Retinopathy

    Recommendation

    • 18 – 39 years
    • Annual screening if one of the following risks:
    • - High normal blood pressure (13 – 139/85 – 89)
    • - Overweight or Obese
    • 40 years and older
    • Annual screening
    • Recommendation
    • Annual Screening
    • In Health Care Setting

    Interpreting the results

    • Systolic < 120 mmHg and
    • Diastolic < 80 mmHg
    • Normal /High Normal (Prehypertension)
    • Systolic 120 – 139 mmHg and/or
    • Diastolic 80 – 89 mmHg
    • Hypertension
    • Systolic > 140 mmHg and/or
    • Diastolic > 90 mmHg

  • Blood Sugar

    Why or What is the risk?

    Diabetes Mellitus is the important risk factor for Cardiovascular Disease (Heart Attack), Cerebrovascular Disease (Stroke), Renal Failure, Peripheral Vascular Disease,Retinopathy

    Recommendation

    • Recommended Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) test any age if one of following risk factors
    • BMI* ≥25 kg/m2(overweight) and additional risk factors:
    • -Physical inactivity
    • -First-degree relative with diabetes
    • -High-risk race/ethnicity
    • -Women who delivered a baby >9 lb or were diagnosed with GDM
    • -HDL-C <35 mg/dL ± TG >250 mg/dL
    • -Hypertension (≥140/90 mm Hg or on therapy)
    • -A1C ≥5.7%, IGT, or IFG on previous testing
    • -Conditions associated with insulin resistance: severe obesity, acanthosis nigricans, PCOS
    • -CVD history
    • 45 or more
    • Every 3 years interval (provided normal blood glucose and no risk factors)
    • Recommendation
    • Annual Screening with FPG in Health Care Setting

    Interpreting the results

    • Normal
    • Fasting plasma glucose (FPG) <100 mg/dl
    • 2 h plasma glucose < 140 mg/dl
    • HbA1c <5.7%
    • Prediabetes
    • FPG 100 mg/dl-125 mg/dl
    • 2 h plasma glucose 140-199 mg/dl
    • HbA1c 5.7%-6.4%
    • Diabetes
    • FPG 126 mg/dl or more
    • 2 h plasma glucose 200 mg/dl or more
    • HbA1c 6.5% or more

  • Weight

    Why or What is the risk?

    Overweight has significant association with Cardiovascular Disease, Diabetes Mellitus, Metabolic Syndrome, Musculoskeletal problems, Cancers.

    Recommendation

    • Annual Screening or
    • More frequently as required

    Interpreting the results

    • Normal weight depends upon height and waist circumference

  • Height

    Why or What is the risk?

    Height is denominator for the calculation of BMI

    Recommendation

    • Height to be measured in meter to calculate BMI

  • Body Mass Index (BMI)

    Why or What is the risk?

    BMI is significantly associated with Cardiovascular Disease, Diabetes Mellitus, Metabolic Syndrome

    Recommendation

    • BMI is calculated as Weight in KG / square of Height in metre
    • Recommended BMI
    • 18.5 – 22.9
    • Recommendation
    • Annual Screening or
    • More frequently as required

    Interpreting the results

    • Underweight < 18.5
    • Normal 18.5 – 22.9
    • Overweight 23 – 24.9
    • Obese 25 – 29.9
    • Morbidly Obese > 30
    • (WHO – Asian population)

  • Waist Circumference

    Why or What is the risk?

    Waist circumference is a better predictor of diabetes.

    Recommendation

    • Measure waist circumference in between top of hip bone and below the bottom rib

    Interpreting the results

    • Optimal Men < 78 cm
    • Women < 72 cm
    • High Men 78 – 89.9 cm
    • Women 72 – 79.9 cm
    • Very High Men >90 cm
    • Women > 80 cm
    • (data for south asian population)

  • Blood Lipid

    Why or What is the risk?

    Lipid panel includes Total Cholesterol, Low density lipoprotein (LDL), High density lipoprotein (HDL), Triglyceerides (TG) High LDL, TG and low HDL are risk factors for cardiovascular disease

    Recommendation

    • Men > 35 years
    • Women > 45 years
    • Universal screening
    • Every 5 years for low CVD risk
    • Every 2 years for intermediate risk or new CVD risk factor
    • (Our contest)
    • Recommendation

    Interpreting the results

    • Normal Level for adult
    • Total Cholesterol < 200 mg/dl
    • LDL < 100 mg/dl
    • HDL > 40 mg/dl
    • TG < 149 mg/dl

  • Dipstic Test (Urinalysis Report)

    Why or What is the risk?

    Dipstick detects protein in urine which is an early indication of kidney damage caused by diabetes, glomerulonephritis, hypertension or other cause.

    Recommendation

    • For all age group once a year. If positive, people should be referred to hospitals for further work-up.

    Interpreting the results

    • It is qualitative test and gives result as negative or positive. (- or +)
